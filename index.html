The Tradition of Thekua

Thekua cookies hold a sacred place in traditional celebrations, particularly during Chhath Puja, one of the most important festivals in Bihar and parts of Nepal. These special cookies are lovingly prepared as an offering to the Sun God.

Our family recipe has been passed down through five generations, starting with our great-great-grandmother who was renowned in her village for making the most delicious Thekua. Today, we continue her legacy by bringing these authentic cookies to cookie lovers around the world.

Each Thekua cookie we make honors both tradition and craftsmanship. The distinctive patterns pressed into each cookie symbolize prosperity and good fortune, making them not just a delicious treat but also a meaningful cultural experience.

How long do the cookies stay fresh?

Our Thekua cookies stay fresh for up to 3 weeks when stored in an airtight container at room temperature. For longer storage, you can refrigerate them for up to 6 weeks or freeze them for up to 3 months.
